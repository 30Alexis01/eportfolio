Sustainability / CSR

This part describes sustainability-related learning and actions, focusing on social responsibility and civic engagement.

Blood donation

  • Context: Personal civic initiative
  • Problem / topic: Supporting healthcare systems and patients in need
  • Actions: Regular blood donation
  • Outcome / impact: Contribution to medical needs through regular donations
  • What I learned:
    • The importance of individual actions in supporting collective needs
    • Regular commitment can have a real and concrete impact

Key sustainability skills

Civic Engagement

This part presents my civic engagement through responsibilities and community involvement, as well as the values and soft skills I developed.

Swimming coach and club involvement

  • Organization: Local swimming club
  • Role: Swimming coach and club support
  • Duration: Several years, ongoing
  • Actions:
    • Coaching and supervising swimmers
    • Occasional volunteer support outside paid hours
    • Sharing technical knowledge and passion for the sport
  • Impact: Support for the club, development of swimmers, and transmission of values such as discipline, respect, and commitment

Values & soft skills

  • Responsibility, reliability, and teamwork
  • Communication, pedagogy, and sense of commitment

Evidence & documents

  • Blood donation certificates or appointment confirmations (if available)
  • Coaching or club involvement attestations (if available)
  • BNSSA and PSE2 certifications

Reflection

  • What I discovered: Civic engagement does not necessarily require large-scale actions; regular and concrete commitments can have a real impact.
  • What challenged me: Taking responsibility for others, especially in safety-related contexts, requires rigor, anticipation, and self-control.
  • How it connects to engineering: Engineering decisions can have direct consequences on people’s safety. Responsibility, prevention, and risk management are therefore essential values.
  • Next steps: Continue contributing to useful projects and, in the future, work on technologies with a concrete positive impact, particularly in safety and rescue contexts.
